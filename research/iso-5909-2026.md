# Research Report: ISO 5909:2026 — Electronic Bill of Lading Based on DLT

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Prepared by:** Alfred
**Source:** ISO 5909:2026(en) document + web research

---

## Overview

ISO 5909:2026 is the first international standard governing the business processes and data interchange of electronic bills of lading (eBL) built on distributed ledger technology (DLT). Published in January 2026 by ISO Technical Committee 154 in collaboration with UN/CEFACT, it defines standardised processes, data structures, and a trust framework for transitioning maritime trade documentation from paper-based to DLT-based systems. The standard is directly relevant to platforms such as Singapore's TradeTrust, which operates in the same domain and leverages compatible blockchain-based document transfer infrastructure.

---

## Key Findings

- **First edition, published January 2026** — ISO 5909 is brand new, developed collaboratively by ISO/TC 154 and UN/CEFACT Transport and Logistics Domain ([ISO](https://www.iso.org/standard/84288.html))
- **Scope:** Electronic bills of lading on DLT platforms — covers both straight B/L and to-order B/L, including multi-party trade scenarios (shippers, carriers, banks, freight forwarders, consignees)
- **Three main functions of a B/L** are formalised: (1) goods receipt, (2) document of title, (3) evidence of contract of carriage
- **DLT is mandated** as the underlying infrastructure — the standard explicitly leverages DLT's immutability, traceability, and tamper-resistance for eBL workflows
- **Data on Chain is a core concept** — Section 7.4 defines the DLT Asset model: structured B/L data is stored directly on the DLT platform, linked by a unique, immutable DLT Asset ID
- **MLETR compliant** — aligned with UNCITRAL Model Law on Electronic Transferable Records, providing the legal foundation for electronic title transfer
- **No formal certification process** — ISO 5909 is a technical compliance and interoperability standard, not a certification scheme (unlike ISO 9001)
- **Real-world precedent:** COSCO SHIPPING has issued 350,000+ eBLs via the GSBN blockchain platform — informed the standard's development

## Deep Dive

### 1. What is ISO 5909?

ISO 5909:2026 is titled: **"Business processes and data interchange of electronic bill of lading based on distributed ledger technology (DLT)"**

It was developed because the maritime industry identified a critical gap: while eBL systems existed, they were fragmented across proprietary platforms with no interoperability or standardised data model. ISO 5909 addresses this by defining:

- A standardised business process for DLT eBL (replacing paper methods)
- A data requirements and interoperability framework
- A trust framework and security mechanism for secure eBL exchange

**ICS Classification:** 35.240.63 (IT applications in trade)
**UN SDG Alignment:** SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth), SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure)

### 4. B/L Types and Business Processes

**Two types of B/L covered:**

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Straight B/L** | Consignee is a specific named party; simpler transfer process |
| **To-Order B/L** | Consignee listed as "to order" — enables continuous endorsement chain (negotiable) |

**Five core eBL actions on the digital platform:**

| Action | Who Can Perform | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Issue** | Carrier only | Creates the eBL; shipper becomes first title owner and holder |
| **Transfer** | Title owner (straight B/L) | Transfers title to named consignee |
| **Endorse** | Title owner (to-order B/L) | Transfers title to endorsee; repeatable multiple times |
| **Escrow / Escrow Release** | Title owner or holder | Authorises a custodian (e.g., bank) as holder without transferring title |
| **Surrender** | Consignee | Terminates the eBL; carrier releases goods |

The platform must maintain a clear, auditable record of both the **title owner** and **holder** at every stage throughout the lifecycle.

---

### 5. Data Structure Overview

The standard defines data elements from two perspectives:

#### A — B/L Body Data (Document View)
Nine segments (B1–B9) based on traditional B/L document layout:

| Segment | Content |
|---|---|
| B1 | Parties (shipper, consignee, carrier, notify party) |
| B2 | Transport details (vessel, voyage, port of loading/discharge) |
| B3 | Cargo/goods description |
| B4 | Container/equipment details |
| B5 | Freight and charges |
| B6 | Dates (issue date, on-board date) |
| B7 | Clauses and special instructions |
| B8 | Signature and authentication |
| B9 | Terms and remarks |

Data element semantics align with **UN/CEFACT MMT-RDM** (Multi-Modal Transport Reference Data Model) and **UNTDED/ISO 7372** (United Nations Trade Data Elements Directory).

#### B — eBL Processing Control (Operational/Functional View)
Covers two sub-views:
- **BOV (Business Operational View):** Foundational B/L information for electronic presentation
- **FSV (Functional Service View):** Additional data elements required by the DLT platform for processing — the "Data on Chain" layer

---

### 6. Data on Chain — Section 7.4 Deep Dive

This is the most significant section for DLT implementation. Section 7.4 ("Generic eBL processing control and record on digital platform") defines what must be stored on the distributed ledger and how.

#### The DLT Asset Model

> *"The DLT Asset is a record on the DLT platform. And for eBL, the DLT asset contains the structured data pertaining to the B/L. During the lifecycle of an eBL, every record (including the processing record, title owner record and B/L holder record) should be linked to its corresponding DLT asset using its unique and immutable DLT asset ID."*
> — ISO 5909:2026, Section 7.4

**This is a fundamental architectural distinction from systems like OpenCerts:** ISO 5909 requires the **actual structured B/L data** to be stored on-chain as a DLT Asset — not just a hash. Every record in the eBL lifecycle is anchored to this asset via its unique DLT Asset ID.

#### Data on Chain — Mandatory Elements (Table 10)

| Data Element | Type | Required | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DLT Asset** | Object | Mandatory | The on-chain record; contains all structured B/L data |
| **DLT Asset ID** | String | Mandatory | Unique, immutable identifier generated by the DLT platform when the eBL is stored. Functions as an index to locate the record. Every eBL has one. |
| **DLT Asset Version** | Integer | Mandatory | Version tracking for the asset |
| **DLT Asset Type** | String | Mandatory | Asset type code (e.g., "eBL") per UNTDED code list |
| **eBL Traceability Log ID** | String | Mandatory | Generated once when the eBL is issued; never changes. Used to trace the entire processing chain across the lifecycle. |

#### Processing Record (On-Chain)

Every action taken on the eBL is recorded on-chain:

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Transaction ID** | Unique reference for each transaction |
| **Time Stamp** | ISO 8601 date/time of the action |
| **Title Processing Action Code** | One of: Issue / Transfer / Endorse / Escrow / Surrender |
| **Action By** | The party who performed the action (recorded as "previous" in title owner record post-action) |
| **Action To** | The party receiving the eBL role |
| **Document Version** | Version of the B/L document |
| **Signatory Text** | Digital signature text |
| **Signatory Image** | Binary signature image |

#### Title Owner Record (On-Chain)

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Current Title Owner** | The party currently holding title of goods |
| **Historical Title Owners** | All previous title owners — full audit trail (1..n occurrences) |
| **Previous Title Owner** | The immediately preceding title owner |

#### B/L Holder Record (On-Chain)

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Current B/L Holder** | The party currently honoured as eBL holder |
| **Historical B/L Holders** | All previous holders — full audit trail (1..n occurrences) |
| **Previous B/L Holder** | The party authorised for escrow or escrow release |

#### Key Design Principles of Data on Chain

1. **Immutability:** The DLT Asset ID is generated once and never changes — it is the permanent anchor for the eBL's entire lifecycle
2. **Completeness:** All processing records, title owner records, and holder records are linked to the DLT Asset — nothing is kept off-chain
3. **Traceability:** The eBL Traceability Log ID persists from issuance to surrender, enabling complete end-to-end audit
4. **Control:** Access and control governed by the title owner at all times; the DLT enforces this cryptographically
5. **Privacy:** The platform must protect cargo details, customer data, and logistics flows from tampering and unauthorised access during transfers

#### What Goes On-Chain vs. What Stays Off-Chain

| On-Chain (DLT Asset) | Off-Chain |
|---|---|
| Structured B/L data | Paper originals (if any) |
| DLT Asset ID (unique, immutable) | Platform-specific UI/presentation layer |
| Processing records (all actions) | Communication channel metadata |
| Title owner records (current + history) | Internal carrier systems |
| Holder records (current + history) | External reference documents |
| Traceability log | |

> **This is a significant contrast to OpenCerts/TradeTrust**, where only a Merkle hash is stored on-chain and the actual document data remains off-chain. ISO 5909 mandates the structured data itself lives on the DLT — enabling full on-chain traceability without relying on off-chain document stores.

---

### 7. Trust Framework and Security

- Each eBL is **uniquely identifiable** and **fully traceable** throughout its lifecycle
- Blockchain broadcasts and records routing information across distributed ledgers during processing
- The entire transaction remains under control of the **sender/title owner**, with access restricted to authorised participants
- Protection against: counterfeiting, tampering, data leakage, and repudiation
- The platform must ensure **authenticity and uniqueness** of the eBL at every stage
- **Customised or proprietary access models are explicitly discouraged** — eBLs must be exchangeable across diverse stakeholders and systems

### 8. Relation to TradeTrust and Singapore Ecosystem

While no explicit documented link between ISO 5909 and TradeTrust/OpenCerts was found in public sources, both share:

| Dimension | ISO 5909 | TradeTrust / OpenCerts |
|---|---|---|
| **Domain** | Maritime eBL on DLT | Trade documents + academic credentials on blockchain |
| **DLT Technology** | Blockchain/DLT (platform-agnostic) | Ethereum / Polygon (public mainnet) |
| **Title Transfer** | Via endorsement chain on DLT | Via NFT token registry (TradeTrust) |
| **MLETR Compliance** | Explicitly compliant | Designed for MLETR compliance |
| **Data on Chain** | Full structured B/L data | Merkle hash only (OpenCerts); NFT token (TradeTrust) |
| **Governing Body** | ISO/TC 154 + UN/CEFACT | GovTech / IMDA Singapore |
| **Standards Alignment** | UN/CEFACT MMT-RDM, UNTDED/ISO 7372 | OpenAttestation, W3C Verifiable Credentials |

**Key difference:** ISO 5909 puts structured data on-chain; TradeTrust's OpenCerts puts only hashes on-chain. TradeTrust's transferable records (eBL equivalent) use NFTs to represent title, which is closer to ISO 5909's DLT Asset model.

### 9. Relation to Other Standards

| Standard | Relationship |

---

### 10. Certification and Implementation

- **No formal ISO certification scheme** exists for ISO 5909
- It is a **technical compliance and interoperability standard** — organisations implement it to ensure their eBL systems meet international requirements
- Compliance is verified through system audits, interoperability testing, and technical assessments
- COSCO SHIPPING implemented compatible systems on GSBN blockchain platform (350,000+ eBLs issued) — demonstrated feasibility before the standard was published

## Recommendations

**For Master Isaac and DEX (TradeTrust context):**

1. **Understand the architectural gap** — ISO 5909 mandates structured B/L data on-chain (the DLT Asset model), whereas TradeTrust/OpenCerts currently stores only hashes (for OpenCerts) or NFT tokens (for eBL). This is a meaningful architectural distinction to be aware of when positioning TradeTrust against ISO 5909 compliance.

2. **TradeTrust eBL alignment** — TradeTrust's transferable records (token registry / NFT-based title transfer) is conceptually aligned with ISO 5909's DLT Asset ID model. There is a strong case to formally map TradeTrust's architecture against ISO 5909 to identify gaps and establish compliance positioning.

3. **IMDA ownership is key** — Since IMDA now maintains OpenCerts (from Oct 2025) and is the Singapore standards body, they are the natural body to drive ISO 5909 adoption or certification positioning in Singapore. The PDF is licensed to an IMDA email (`seetoh_chee_whye@imda.gov.sg`) — IMDA is already engaged with this standard.

4. **Interoperability opportunity** — ISO 5909's emphasis on cross-platform eBL interoperability opens an opportunity for TradeTrust to position itself as an ISO 5909-compatible platform, increasing international adoption.

5. **Data residency consideration** — ISO 5909's on-chain data model (full structured data on DLT) creates a more acute data residency challenge than OpenCerts' hash-only model. If TradeTrust aims for ISO 5909 compliance, the Ethereum mainnet residency question (raised in the IM8 compliance report) becomes even more critical to resolve.
